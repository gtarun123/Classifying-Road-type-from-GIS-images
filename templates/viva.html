🧠 1. Convolutional Layer Mechanics

CNNs utilize sliding filters (kernels) to execute spatial convolutions. These filters multiply their weight matrices against localized pixel coordinates, yielding feature maps that isolate vital outlines, highway lanes, elevated flyovers, and background vegetation boundary markers.

🧬 2. Rationale of Transfer Learning

Instead of training millions of randomized weights from scratch, Transfer Learning imports parameters pre-trained on the high-variety ImageNet dataset. By freezing these features, we preserve complex shape extractors, avoiding overfitting and reducing training times by 90%.

⚡ 3. Lightweight Edge-AI Convolutions

MobileNetV2 implements **Depthwise Separable Convolutions** which split standard 3D filters into a spatial 2D Depthwise convolution and a 1x1 Pointwise channel-merger. This mathematical separation reduces mathematical calculations by approximately 8.5 times, providing rapid CPU responses.
